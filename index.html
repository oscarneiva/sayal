About

In Cape Breton, Nova Scotia, on the Yankee Line at the foot of Hunter’s Mountain stands a small rundown old cabin. The cabin once belonged to Elliot Hurts, the bass player in a prolific yet relatively unknown local rock band, SALE.

When James Timmins a.k.a Shipzy, a senior change-management consultant from the UK purchased the property in 2016 with the intention of turning it into a yoga retreat for business executives, he found all of Elliot’s worldly possessions still inside the property. Hanging on a hook, just to the right of the fireplace was a cheap metallic necklace featuring an image of St George. Despite the numerous items in the cabin this one in particular called out to James Timmins. He put the necklace on, and still to this day, more than five years later has never taken it off. Slowly, but surely over a period of months, his whole outlook on life began change.

He would have flashbacks to times and places that he had never experienced, he would imagine performing on stage, he had a joie de vivre that was completely unfamiliar to him. On returning to Rio de Janeiro, where he was living and continues to reside he decided to research the band SALE further and found pictures of their singer Desmond Escargot wearing the exact same necklace. He had the compulsion to start a SALE cover band; could the spirit of this long dead singer somehow have entered his body via the necklace? He would now need to find band members to join him on this voyage.

One evening at the Yacht Club where James Timmins would go to sip G&Ts after a hard day of Senior Change-Management he got speaking to a young seaman by the name of O’Harold, a salty dog, from a long line of neerdowell seafarers. O’Harold had played many a shanty and felt a kinship to the mysterious tale of Elliot and Desmond, and so agreed to join. The pair in need of more recruits put posters on every lamppost in Ipanema, “Lost parrot, R$10000 Reward”. Over one hundred people came forward with lost parrots, but out of all of them Luiza was by far the best bass player, and thus was hired.

Now a trio they started rehearsing, and it was great, but still the spirit of Desmond Escargot wasn’t totally at peace. Luckily fate intervened one Monday morning in October 2020. The three of them were playing Truco and sipping Original outside a luncheonette in Bairro Peixoto whilst a random array of objects were being thrown from an apartment window above them. One item, a relatively large teapot struck a passer by. Shipzy, O’Harold & Luiza went to help the handsome mysterious stranger. Such was his skill in Truco that this good looking Pernambucan was asked to join the band as lead guitarist and thus SAYAL was born.
