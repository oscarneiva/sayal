ABOUT

In Cape Breton, Nova Scotia, on the Yankee Line at the foot of Hunter’s Mountain stands a small rundown old cabin. The cabin once belonged to Elliot Hurts, the bass player in a prolific, yet relatively unknown local rock band, SALE.

When in 2016, James Timmins a.k.a Shipzy, a senior change-management consultant from the UK purchased the land with the intention of turning it into a yoga retreat for business executives, he found all of Elliot’s worldly possessions still inside the property. Hanging on a hook, to the right of the fireplace was a cheap metallic necklace featuring an image of St George. Despite the numerous items in the cabin this one in particular called out to James Timmins. He immediately put the necklace on, and still to this day, more than five years later is yet to take it off.

Slowly, but surely, over a period of months his whole outlook on life changed. He would have flashbacks to times and places that he had never experienced, he would imagine performing on stage, he had a joie de vivre that was completely unfamiliar to him. On returning to Rio de Janeiro, where he presently resides he decided to research SALE further, and remarkably found pictures of their singer, Desmond Escargot wearing the same item of jewelry. He now had the compulsion to start a SALE cover band; was it simply serendipity or could the spirit of this long-dead singer somehow have entered his body via the necklace? Having limited musical experience himself, he would need to find band members to join him on this orphic voyage.

One evening at the Yacht Club where James Timmins would go to sip G&Ts after a hard day of Senior Change-Management he got chatting to a young marinor by the name of O’Harold, a salty dog from a long line of neerdowell seafarers. O’Harold had played many a shanty and felt a sudden kinship to the mysterious tale of Elliot and Desmond, and so agreed to join the endeavour.

The pair in need of more recruits put posters on every lamppost in Ipanema, “Lost parrot, R$10,000 Reward”. Over one hundred people came forward with lost parrots, but out of all of them Luiza was by far the best bass player, and thus was hired on the spot.

Now a trio they started rehearsing, but although they were making a joyous racket, still the spirit of Desmond Escargot wasn’t at peace. Luckily fate intervened one Monday morning in October 2020. The three of them were playing Truco and sipping Original outside a luncheonette in Bairro Peixoto whilst a random array of objects were being hurled from an apartment window above. One item, a relatively large teapot struck a passer by. Shipzy, O’Harold & Luiza went to the aid of the mysterious stranger, and such was his skill in Truco that this suave Prenambucan named Vigulino was asked to join the crew as lead guitarist. SAYAL WAS BORN!
